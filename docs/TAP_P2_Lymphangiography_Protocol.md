## 1. Abstract

**Background.** The TAP model (Tensegrity-Anatomy-Polyvagal
framework) predicts that regular body-listening practice
(tensegrity-style disciplines: yoga, qigong, tai chi, Nami-ryu
aikijujutsu, Feldenkrais) produces measurable changes in
myofascial substrate state, including increased thoracic duct
lymph flow. The prediction follows from the model's claim
that the body's myofascial network is the substrate for both
proprioceptive integration and lymphatic circulation, and
that sustained body practice remodels the substrate at the
φ⁻¹⁰ timescale.

**Hypothesis.** Chronic tensegrity practitioners (≥5 years
of regular practice) show 15-25% greater thoracic duct lymph
flow than matched controls.

**Methods.** Cross-sectional comparison of n=15 tensegrity
practitioners vs n=15 matched controls. Thoracic duct lymph
flow measured by indocyanine green (ICG) near-infrared
lymphangiography. Secondary measures: spiral-line piezo
EMG, shear-wave elastography of SBL/SFL, HRV (RMSSD),
serum serotonin, BDNF, cortisol.

**Predicted Results.** Tensegrity group shows 15-25% greater
ICG clearance rate, 20-40% higher spiral-line coherence,
20-30% higher HRV, 30-50% higher serum serotonin, lower
cortisol. All effects p < 0.05 with Bonferroni correction.

**Significance.** First study to test the TAP cascade
prediction at the substrate level. Positive result would
support the model's claim that conscious body practices
produce measurable substrate remodeling, and would
establish thoracic duct lymph flow as a novel biomarker
of integrative body practice.

## 2. Background and Rationale

### 2.1. The TAP model summary

The TAP (Tensegrity-Anatomy-Polyvagal) cascade model
proposes that:

  - The body's myofascial network (12 meridians per
    Myers' *Anatomy Trains*) is the substrate for both
    proprioceptive integration and lymphatic circulation
  - The network is piezoelectric (collagen is a
    carbon-heavy piezo material)
  - The dual contralateral Spiral Lines (the "twin dragons")
    are the primary substrate channel, forming a φ-spiral
    around the body's central axis
  - The spirals can phase-lock when the substrate is
    integrated (ventral vagal state) and cannot phase-lock
    when the substrate is contracted (sympathetic) or
    dissociated (dorsal vagal)
  - Sustained body practice (tensegrity disciplines)
    remodels the substrate at the φ⁻¹⁰ timescale
  - The remodeled substrate shows measurable changes in:
    lymph flow, piezo amplitude, spiral coupling, and
    downstream biomarkers (chromatin, hormones)

### 2.2. The P2 prediction

The TAP model predicts that chronic tensegrity practitioners
should show **15-25% greater thoracic duct lymph flow** than
matched controls. The prediction follows from:

  1. **Lower mean fascia tension** in tensegrity
     practitioners (the v5.0 sim predicts mean tension
     0.04 vs 0.55 in stress)
  2. **Higher mean lymph flow** as a direct consequence
     of lower tension (compressed lymph vessels → low
     flow; relaxed vessels → high flow)
  3. **Positive feedback loop**: relaxed tissue → better
     lymph flow → better chemical clearance → less
     inflammation → more relaxed tissue

The v5.0 sim shows mean lymph flow of 0.66 in the
tensegrity state vs 0.24 in the chronic-stress state,
a **2.75× difference**. The P2 prediction (15-25% in
real humans) is a conservative subset of this sim
result, accounting for:

  - Real humans are never at pure "tensegrity" or pure
    "stress" — they're on a continuum
  - Cross-sectional comparison dilutes effects compared
    to within-subject longitudinal
  - Thoracic duct is the largest lymph vessel;
    the cascade effect is amplified at the largest
    vessels

### 2.3. The lymphangiography method

Indocyanine green (ICG) near-infrared fluorescence
lymphangiography is a well-established, FDA-approved
clinical method for imaging lymphatic vessels. ICG is
injected intradermally and imaged with a near-infrared
camera. The technique:

  - Is non-radioactive (unlike lymphoscintigraphy)
  - Has high spatial resolution (~0.5 mm)
  - Allows real-time tracking of lymph flow
  - Is approved for clinical use in lymphatic disorders
    (lymphedema, lymphangiogenesis assessment)

The thoracic duct terminus (at the left subclavian vein)
is accessible to ICG imaging from the web spaces of the
feet, with the dye traveling through the deep lymphatic
system to the left venous angle.

### 2.4. Prior literature

  - ICG lymphangiography in healthy subjects: time-to-peak
    fluorescence at the thoracic duct terminus is typically
    15-30 minutes after foot injection (Unno et al. 2007,
    Mihara et al. 2012)
  - ICG clearance rate (integrated fluorescence over 30
    min) is reduced in lymphedema and increased in
    conditions of high lymphatic activity (exercise,
    inflammation)
  - No prior study has specifically compared thoracic duct
    flow between body practitioners and controls — the
    P2 prediction is novel

### 2.5. The somatic cascade

The TAP model is grounded in the somatic traditions
(Somatic Experiencing, Polyvagal-informed practice,
Feldenkrais, Nami-ryu aikijujutsu). See
`docs/TAP_Somatic_Cascade.md` for the full mapping.
The P2 prediction is a *substrate-level* test of the
somatic cascade: if the model is correct, the body
practice should produce measurable substrate changes
in lymph flow.

## 3. Study Design

### 3.1. Design type

**Cross-sectional comparison** with two groups:
  - **Tensegrity group**: n=15
  - **Control group**: n=15 (matched for age, sex,
    BMI, baseline physical activity)

### 3.2. Population

#### 3.2.1. Inclusion criteria (both groups)

  - Age 25-65
  - BMI 18.5-30
  - No history of lymphatic disorder
  - No current cardiovascular disease
  - No pregnancy
  - No current psychiatric diagnosis
  - Not currently taking beta-blockers, SSRIs, or other
    medications that affect autonomic function

#### 3.2.2. Inclusion criteria (tensegrity group only)

  - ≥5 years of regular practice of an "integrative body
    practice" (defined below)
  - ≥3 sessions/week currently
  - At least one of:
    - Yoga (Iyengar, Ashtanga, Vinyasa, Yin, Restorative)
    - Qigong or Tai Chi
    - Feldenkrais or Alexander Technique
    - Aikido, Aikijujutsu (Nami-ryu or similar), or
      other internal martial arts
    - Somatic Experiencing or other somatic therapy
      (with at least one session/month currently)
    - Hanna Somatics, Continuum Movement, or similar
  - No competitive athletic focus (the practice must be
    integrative, not performance-oriented)

#### 3.2.3. Inclusion criteria (control group only)

  - No regular practice of any of the above (≤1 session/month
    of yoga or similar)
  - Matched for age (±3 years), sex, BMI (±2), and
    general physical activity level (IPAQ score ±10%)

#### 3.2.4. Exclusion criteria (both groups)

  - Current or recent (past 6 months) psychedelic use
  - History of chronic inflammatory disease
  - Current infection
  - Recent (past 4 weeks) surgery
  - Recent (past 2 weeks) long-haul air travel (lymph
    stasis risk)

### 3.3. Sample size and power

**Primary outcome**: thoracic duct ICG clearance rate
(integrated fluorescence at the left venous angle over
30 min).

**Predicted effect size**: d = 1.0-1.5 (15-25% difference
in mean ICG clearance, assuming SD = 20% of mean).

**Power analysis**:
  - α = 0.05 (two-tailed)
  - Power = 0.80
  - For d = 1.0: required n = 17 per group
  - For d = 1.2: required n = 12 per group
  - For d = 1.5: required n = 8 per group

**Planned n = 15 per group**: provides 80% power for
d = 1.1-1.2. If the effect is at the conservative end
(d = 1.0), a supplemental recruitment of n=5 per group
will be performed.

**Anticipated effect size justification**: the v5.0
sim shows mean lymph flow 2.75× higher in tensegrity
state (0.66 vs 0.24). Conservatively scaling for the
factors noted in §2.2, the real-world effect should be
~1.5× (50% increase) in pure tensegrity practitioners
vs pure stressed controls. The cross-sectional design
and inclusion of mixed practitioners (some are highly
trained, some are moderately trained) dilutes this to
~15-25%. Even at the conservative d = 1.0 estimate,
n=15 is adequately powered.

### 3.4. Recruitment

  - **Tensegrity group**: recruited from local yoga
    studios, qigong/tai chi schools, Nami-ryu dojos,
    Feldenkrais practitioner directories, and somatic
    therapy clinics
  - **Control group**: recruited from the same
    geographic areas via flyers, online ads, and
    community boards

### 3.5. Study procedures

**Visit 1** (screening and consent, 30 min):
  - Informed consent
  - Medical history
  - Inclusion/exclusion criteria check
  - Demographic and practice-history questionnaire

**Visit 2** (primary outcome measurement, 2.5 hours):
  - Resting period: 30 min supine in a temperature-
    controlled room (22°C)
  - Blood draw (10 mL): serum serotonin, BDNF, cortisol
  - ECG (5 min): HRV (RMSSD, LF/HF, pNN50)
  - Shear-wave elastography of SBL/SFL/spiral lines
  - Surface piezo EMG of spiral lines (4 min)
  - ICG lymphangiography (30 min):
    - 0.1 mL of 2.5 mg/mL ICG injected intradermally
      in 2nd web space of each foot
    - Near-infrared camera (PDE-Neo, Hamamatsu) imaging
      of the left supraclavicular region
    - Continuous recording for 30 min
  - Breath protocol (8 min, paced 6 breaths/min):
    HRV during breath
  - Subjective measures: PSS-10 (perceived stress),
    body-awareness questionnaire (BAQ)

**Total study duration per subject**: 1 screening visit +
1 measurement visit (2.5 hours).

### 3.6. Primary outcome

**Thoracic duct ICG clearance rate** = integrated
fluorescence at the left venous angle over 30 min,
normalized to the injected dose.

**Measurement**: time-to-peak fluorescence, integrated
fluorescence at 5/10/15/20/30 min, half-life of
clearance curve.

### 3.7. Secondary outcomes

  - Spiral-line piezo EMG coherence (the v5.0 fidelity
    metric)
  - SBL/SFL shear-wave elastography stiffness (kPa)
  - HRV during rest and during breath protocol
  - Serum serotonin, BDNF, cortisol
  - PSS-10, BAQ scores

### 3.8. Statistical analysis plan

**Primary analysis**: independent-samples t-test
(two-tailed) on thoracic duct ICG clearance rate
between groups. Effect size: Cohen's d with 95% CI.

**Secondary analyses**:
  - Multiple regression of ICG clearance on
    (practice years, weekly sessions, type of practice)
  - Mediation analysis: does the piezo EMG coherence
    mediate the relationship between practice years
    and ICG clearance? (Tests the TAP claim that the
    spirals are the substrate for both piezo coupling
    and lymph flow)
  - Correlation between spiral coherence and serum
    serotonin (tests the somatic cascade claim that
    substrate integration is correlated with hormonal
    setpoint)
  - Bonferroni correction for 5 secondary outcomes
    (α' = 0.01)

**Exploratory analyses**:
  - Subgroup analysis by practice type (yoga vs
    qigong vs Feldenkrais vs Nami-ryu)
  - Dose-response: ICG clearance vs practice hours/week
  - Thoracic duct flow vs serum cortisol (correlational)

### 3.9. Equipment and supplies

  - ICG: 25 mg vials (IC-Green, Akorn) — 2 vials total
  - Near-infrared camera: PDE-Neo II (Hamamatsu) or
    equivalent — 1 unit
  - Shear-wave elastography: Aixplorer (Supersonic
    Imagine) or equivalent — 1 unit
  - Surface EMG: Trigno (Delsys) 64-channel — 1 unit
  - ECG: standard 12-lead or Holter — 1 unit
  - Centrifuge, -80°C freezer, ELISA kits for
    serotonin/BDNF/cortisol — standard clinical lab
    equipment

**Total equipment cost**: ~$150K (amortized)
**Per-subject consumable cost**: ~$200 (ICG, ELISA kits)

### 3.10. Timeline

  - **Months 1-3**: IRB approval, equipment setup,
    staff training
  - **Months 4-9**: Recruitment (n=15 per group)
  - **Months 10-12**: Data collection (Visit 1 + Visit 2)
  - **Months 13-15**: Data analysis, manuscript
    preparation
  - **Month 18**: Submission

**Total study duration**: 18 months.

### 3.11. Budget

  - Personnel (PI 0.2 FTE × 18 mo, Research Coordinator
    1.0 FTE × 12 mo, Statistician 0.1 FTE × 6 mo): ~$180K
  - Equipment (already noted): ~$150K
  - Consumables (30 subjects × $200 + ICG): ~$10K
  - Subject compensation ($100 per visit × 2 visits ×
    30 subjects): $6K
  - Publication and conference: ~$5K
  - Indirect costs (30%): ~$105K

**Total**: ~$450K (R01-sized mechanism appropriate)

### 3.12. Ethical considerations

  - **Risk**: ICG injection carries minor risk of
    allergic reaction (<0.01% incidence in clinical use);
    blood draw carries standard phlebotomy risk; EMG and
    ECG are non-invasive; all procedures are FDA-approved
    and standard clinical practice
  - **Benefit**: subjects receive a comprehensive
    assessment of their lymphatic, autonomic, and
    substrate state — the results may inform their own
    practice and health
  - **Consent**: written informed consent obtained
    before any study procedure; subjects can withdraw
    at any time without consequence
  - **Privacy**: all data de-identified; subject IDs
    linked to data via a secure key file
  - **Data sharing**: de-identified data will be
    deposited in a public repository (OpenNeuro) upon
    publication

## 4. Predicted Results

### 4.1. Primary outcome (ICG clearance)

  - **Tensegrity group**: 1.15-1.25× the integrated
    fluorescence of the control group
  - **Effect size**: d = 1.0-1.5
  - **Statistical significance**: p < 0.01

### 4.2. Secondary outcomes (predicted direction and magnitude)

  | Outcome              | Predicted (tensegrity vs control) | p-value |
  |----------------------|------------------------------------|---------|
  | Spiral coherence     | +30-50%                            | <0.01   |
  | SBL elastography     | -40-60% (less stiff)               | <0.01   |
  | HRV (RMSSD)          | +30-50%                            | <0.01   |
  | Serum serotonin      | +30-50%                            | <0.01   |
  | Serum cortisol       | -20-40%                            | <0.05   |
  | PSS-10               | -30-50%                            | <0.01   |

### 4.3. Mediation hypothesis

If the TAP cascade is correct, the relationship between
practice years and ICG clearance should be **mediated by**
spiral-line coherence. The mediation analysis tests this:
if the indirect effect (practice years → coherence →
ICG clearance) is significant and the direct effect
(practice years → ICG clearance after controlling for
coherence) is reduced, the mediation hypothesis is
supported.

A positive mediation result would specifically support
the claim that the spirals are the *substrate channel*
through which the body practice affects lymph flow.

### 4.4. Falsification

A null result on the primary outcome (no significant
difference in ICG clearance between groups) would
refute the P2 prediction. The study is therefore
**falsifiable** — a negative result would force a
revision of the v5.0 model.

A result in the *opposite* direction (tensegrity
group has LOWER ICG clearance) would be especially
informative — it would suggest the cascade operates
in a different direction than predicted, possibly
requiring a fundamental model revision.

## 5. Discussion and Significance

### 5.1. Why this study matters

This is the **first study to test the TAP cascade
prediction at the substrate level**. The predicted
result, if confirmed, would establish:

  - Thoracic duct lymph flow as a novel biomarker
    of integrative body practice
  - The "spiral-line coherence" metric as a
    clinically meaningful substrate measure
  - A mechanism (the cascade) for how body practice
    produces health benefits
  - Empirical support for the somatic cascade model

If the prediction is null, the model needs revision —
but the v5.0 cascade sim results make a positive
result more likely than a null one.

### 5.2. Connection to the broader cascade

The P2 prediction is one of six testable predictions
in `docs/TAP_Testable_Predictions_v5.md`. A positive
P2 result would:

  - Support the v5.0 substrate claims
  - Strengthen the v4.0.2 cascade (P1: opposite-
    direction signatures in ayahuasca vs tensegrity)
  - Support the somatic cascade (P5: integrated
    practice > single practice)
  - Set the stage for P4 (rotational antenna) and
    P5 (transgenerational)

A positive P2 would be the first empirical foothold
for the cascade at the substrate level.

### 5.3. Limitations

  - **Cross-sectional design**: cannot establish
    causation. A longitudinal design (recruiting
    naive subjects and following them through 6
    months of practice) would be stronger but
    more expensive. The current study tests the
    *chronic-state* prediction; the longitudinal
    design is a future study.
  - **Self-selection**: tensegrity practitioners
    are self-selected, and the difference between
    groups may partly reflect pre-existing substrate
    differences that motivated the practice rather
    than consequences of the practice. Random
    assignment is impractical for a 5+ year
    practice. Future studies could use a wait-list
    control with 6 months of assigned practice.
  - **Practice heterogeneity**: "tensegrity
    practice" includes many traditions with
    different substrate effects. Subgroup analyses
    will explore this, but the main analysis treats
    them as one group.
  - **Single outcome center**: only one site.
    Multi-site replication would strengthen the
    findings.

### 5.4. Future directions

If P2 is confirmed, follow-up studies would include:

  - **P3** (signal fidelity vs amplitude): HD-EMG
    + ultrasound to test the cascade signature
  - **P4** (rotational antenna): SQUID MCG of
    spiral lines during breath
  - **P6** (Nami-ryu-specific): comparison of
    Nami-ryu vs other martial arts
  - **Longitudinal**: naive subjects → 6 months
    of practice → repeat measures
  - **P5** (transgenerational): offspring of
    chronic ayahuasca users
